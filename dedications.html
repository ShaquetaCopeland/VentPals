Founders’ Dedications

A Mother’s Emotional Lineage

Six real children. Six emotional arcs. Six birds who carry their stories — from oldest to youngest.

Each dedication includes their percher bird and a place for their photo. This is the emotional backbone of VentPals.

Desmond
Sable — Resilience & Strength
The boy who carried more than most and grew into grounded strength.

Sable is dedicated to Desmond — a boy who grew up navigating two worlds, was hurt and sometimes hurt others, and still chose to become a respectful, emotionally mature man.

Sable represents:

  • Navigating complicated family dynamics
  • Being bullied, then becoming the bully — and growing past it
  • Learning from every mistake
  • Breaking cycles instead of repeating them
  • Turning pain into strength and wisdom

Sable is the bird who sits with hard truths and teaches that strength isn’t loud — it’s earned. Desmond earned every feather.

Dylan
Lumen — Insight & Clarity
A brilliant, different mind building his own map.

Lumen is dedicated to Dylan — a boy with a bright, nonlinear mind who was never broken, only mismatched with systems that didn’t know how to teach him.

Lumen represents:

  • Learning outside the lines
  • Being mislabeled because adults didn’t understand
  • Needing a different kind of teaching and pace
  • Feeling “behind” while actually on a different timeline
  • Searching for meaning and self‑understanding

Lumen is the bird who whispers, “You’re not behind — you were never given the right map.” Dylan is still building his, and that is beautiful.

Samarah
Echo — Validation & Quiet Feelings
A bright star who carries her storms in silence.

Echo is dedicated to Samarah — a strong‑minded, social, radiant girl who rarely appears upset, yet feels deeply and keeps most of it inside.

Echo represents:

  • Feeling everything but showing almost nothing
  • Protecting others from her own pain
  • Being praised for being “strong” and “easy”
  • Not wanting to be a burden or “too much”
  • Longing to be understood without having to perform

Echo is the bird who says, “You don’t have to say it out loud for it to matter.” Samarah deserves to be seen gently, fully, without having to ask.

Junior
Flicker — Hope & Curiosity
A blazing spark of big energy and big love.

Flicker is dedicated to Junior — loud, fast, full of love and excitement, often misunderstood as “too much” in environments that move slower than he does.

Flicker represents:

  • Big energy and big feelings
  • Acting out when he feels invisible or unwanted
  • Being smart but unstimulated by slow schoolwork
  • Looking for attention because he’s craving affection
  • Having a heart that loves everyone, even when he doesn’t feel loved back

Flicker is the bird who says, “You’re not too much — you’re a spark.” Junior’s brightness is a gift, not a problem.

Sarahbi
Twig — Early Growth & Inner Worlds
A quiet builder of worlds, music, and patterns.

Twig is dedicated to Sarahbi — a quiet, self‑entertained creator who plays, builds, stacks, and sings her way through her own imaginative universe.

Twig represents:

  • Self‑directed growth and learning by doing
  • Finding comfort in solitude and imagination
  • Protecting her focus and not wanting to be interrupted
  • Being soothed by music, sound, and background noise
  • Remembering every rhyme, pattern, and tune

Twig is the bird who says, “I grow in my own way, in my own time.” Sarahbi is quiet magic in motion.

SaKovia
Nova — Perspective & Becoming
A bright little soul still finding her place.

Nova is dedicated to SaKovia — a brave, premature‑born girl with a big voice who is still trying on the world, borrowing from siblings while she searches for her own path.

Nova represents:

  • Not yet knowing exactly who you are — and that being okay
  • Trying on different styles, interests, and personalities
  • Feeling left out while wanting to belong
  • Being smart but feeling the need to prove it
  • Learning she doesn’t have to impress anyone to be loved

Nova is the bird who says, “You’re allowed to grow. You’re allowed to become.” SaKovia is the baby of the family, already loved, learning she doesn’t have to earn her place.
